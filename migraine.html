How many days a month do you take something for headache pain?

Any pain reliever, over-the-counter or prescription, including Tylenol, Advil, Aleve, or triptans.

Why we ask Taking acute pain medication too often can actually cause more headaches, a common, reversible problem called medication overuse headache. The tipping point depends on the medication: triptans, opioids, and combination painkillers can cause it at around 10 days a month, while plain Tylenol or anti-inflammatories take about 15. If you're over that line, more acute medication isn't the answer, a reset paired with a preventive plan is.
