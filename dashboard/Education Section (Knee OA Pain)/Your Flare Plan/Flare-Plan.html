Your Flare Plan: calm an angry knee

Flares happen, and they settle. A short, simple plan for the rough days: what to do at home, and when to get checked instead.

From your Clara care team 3 min read One page plan Checked against current guidelines

Flares catch you at home, on an ordinary day. Here is what to do next.

A flare is a temporary rise in your usual knee pain, often after a busier day than normal. It is not a sign of new damage, and most flares settle within days. Here is the plan.

1

Cool it down

For a flare with extra pain or swelling, an ice pack wrapped in a thin towel, in short stretches a few times a day, can take the edge off, especially after the activity that stirred it up(1). Once the worst settles, a heat pack or hot water bottle can ease pain and stiffness(1).

2

Ease, do not stop

Cut your usual activity roughly in half for a few days, or switch to the pool or a stationary bike, which carry the load for you. Resting completely tends to backfire, gentle movement keeps the joint settled(2). Ease back in over about a week: half your routine, then most of it, then full.

3

Lean on your cream

Keep using your prescribed cream right on the knee. Topical anti-inflammatories are the first choice for knee pain, with effects comparable to pills and far less reaching the rest of your body(1, 3).

4

A short course of an oral anti-inflammatory, if cleared for you

If your physician has already cleared an oral anti-inflammatory (an NSAID such as naproxen or ibuprofen), a short course taken as directed, with food, can help a stubborn flare(1, 4). Skip this step if you have been told to avoid NSAIDs, for example for stomach, kidney, heart, or blood pressure reasons, and message Clara if you are not sure(4).

Flares are a detour, not a setback. Cool it, ease off a little, lean on your cream, and let it settle. Your knee has not lost the ground you have gained.

When to see a doctor instead

Stop the home plan and get care in person if your knee suddenly becomes hot, red, or very swollen, if you develop a fever, if you cannot put weight on the knee, if it locks or gives way, or if you have new pain or swelling in your calf. Please see your family doctor or an urgent clinic.

Also message your Clara care team if the flare has not started settling after about a week on this plan, if this flare feels very different from your usual pain, or if flares are coming more often. That is a signal to review your plan, not to push through.
