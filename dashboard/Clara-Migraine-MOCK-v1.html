The clara difference

Most places hand you a pill. We start by explaining your migraine.

A migraine is not just a bad headache. It moves through phases, and it has its own triggers and pattern for you. Before any prescription, clara helps you see what is actually happening, because a migraine you understand is a migraine you can get ahead of.

Phase 1

Prodrome

Subtle early signs hours to a day before: mood shifts, food cravings, yawning, a stiff neck.

Phase 2

Aura

For some, visual or sensory changes, flickers, blind spots or tingling, usually under an hour.

Phase 3

Headache

The attack itself: often one sided and throbbing, with nausea and sensitivity to light and sound.

Phase 4

Postdrome

The hangover after: drained, foggy and tender for up to a day once the pain lifts.

What we treat with

Real options, matched to you.

clara prescribes the clinically proven migraine medications, and a doctor decides which ones fit your history. Three ways to take on a migraine:

Stop an attack

Acute relief

Taken when an attack starts, to cut it short. A doctor starts gentle and steps up only if needed.

  • Anti inflammatories ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, celecoxib
  • Triptans sumatriptan, rizatriptan, eletriptan
  • Gepants ubrogepant (Ubrelvy), rimegepant (Nurtec)
Prevent the next one

Daily prevention

For frequent or severe migraines, a daily medication to make attacks rarer and milder over time.

  • Amitriptyline low dose, taken at night
  • Venlafaxine an SNRI option
  • Botox for chronic migraine, 15+ days a month

Good questions

Migraine, answered.

How do I know it is a migraine and not just a headache?

Migraine is its own neurological condition, not simply a strong headache. It is often one sided and throbbing, lasts anywhere from a few hours to a few days, and usually comes with nausea or sensitivity to light and sound. Our assessment walks through the diagnostic criteria, and a doctor confirms it before recommending anything.

What can clara actually prescribe for migraine?

For stopping an attack: anti inflammatories like ibuprofen or naproxen, triptans like sumatriptan, rizatriptan and eletriptan, and newer gepants like Ubrelvy and Nurtec. For prevention: daily options such as amitriptyline or venlafaxine, and Botox for chronic migraine. A doctor decides which are safe and right for you.

Can migraines be prevented, not just treated?

Often, yes. If your attacks are frequent or disabling, a daily preventive medication can make them rarer and milder. clara also helps you map and manage your personal triggers, because lifestyle changes and medication work best together.

Do I need a video appointment?

Usually not. clara uses an asynchronous model, so you complete your assessment on your own time and a doctor reviews it, typically within 24 hours. If they need more information, they will message you securely or arrange a quick call.

Is this safe, and does clara use opioids?

Never. clara is built on modern, non opioid migraine care. Every plan is reviewed by a licensed Canadian doctor who screens your full history and contraindications, and we actively watch for medication overuse that can cause rebound headaches.

Can I transfer or refill an existing prescription?

Yes. If a migraine medication is already working for you, tell us during your assessment and a doctor can continue it where appropriate, and your prescription can be filled through clara's pharmacy.

When should I see someone in person instead?

Some symptoms need urgent, in person care, like a sudden severe headache unlike any before, headache with fever and a stiff neck, weakness or trouble speaking. Our assessment screens for these, and if anything looks concerning we will tell you to seek in person care right away.
