Is the skin where you would apply the cream healthy and unbroken?

Look at the area you would treat. We mean the skin surface itself, not the pain underneath.

Why we askThis is the single most important safety check for any compounded cream. Applying it to broken, open, blistered, ulcerated, raw, or infected skin lets far too much medicine into the body and has caused serious reactions. If your skin is not intact right now, we wait until it heals.

Let's wait until your skin has healed.

Compounded creams must only go on healthy, unbroken skin. On broken, open, blistered, or infected skin, far too much of the medicine is absorbed into the body, which can cause serious side effects. This is the most important rule for using these creams safely.

What to do now:
If you have an active shingles rash, a wound, or an ulcer over the area, let it fully heal first, and have any infection treated by your doctor. Once the skin is intact, come back and we will build your plan. If you are unsure, your clara doctor is happy to advise.

Let's have a doctor manage this in person.

Our starter creams, N1 and N2, contain an anti-inflammatory called diclofenac. With severe heart failure or advanced kidney disease, even a topical anti-inflammatory can make those conditions worse, so clara does not dispense these creams to you online.

What to do now:
Please work with your doctor or specialist to manage your nerve pain safely alongside your heart or kidney condition. They can decide whether a different, anti-inflammatory free option is right for you.

Let's hold off while you're pregnant.

Our nerve pain creams contain amitriptyline, ketamine, gabapentin, and an anti-inflammatory (diclofenac), among other agents. There is not enough safety information for these applied to the skin during pregnancy, and anti-inflammatories are not used in later pregnancy, so clara does not dispense them while you are pregnant or might be.

What to do now:
Please speak with your own doctor or obstetric provider about pain options that are right for pregnancy. After pregnancy, come back and we will build your plan. If you are breastfeeding rather than pregnant, you can return and choose that option for a doctor reviewed plan.
