What happens

Good questions

Knee pain, answered.

How do I know my knee pain is osteoarthritis?

Osteoarthritis usually builds gradually, with stiffness after rest, pain that is worse with activity or stairs, and sometimes swelling or a grinding feeling. Our assessment walks through the diagnostic criteria, and a doctor confirms it and rules out other causes before recommending anything. If you are ever unsure about having knee osteoarthritis, please check with your family doctor.

Why does clara lead with a cream instead of pills?

Because the guidelines do. Topical anti inflammatories are a recommended first line for knee osteoarthritis, with relief comparable to oral medication and far less reaching the rest of your body. A compound cream lets a doctor combine and titrate proven ingredients to your specific knee.

Does exercise really help, or will it wear my knee out faster?

Exercise is the single most effective treatment for knee osteoarthritis, and the right kind protects the joint rather than wearing it down. clara gives you gentle, guided walking, strengthening and tai chi programs designed specifically for sore knees.

Do I need a video appointment?

Usually not. clara uses an asynchronous model, so you complete your assessment on your own time and a doctor reviews it, typically within 24 hours. If they need more information, they will message you securely or arrange a quick call.

What kind of medications does clara use?

clara starts with the lowest risk option that works: a topical compound cream applied right at the knee, so less reaches your bloodstream than with pills. When more is needed, a doctor can add anti-inflammatories or other proven medications, at the lowest effective dose. Every plan is reviewed by a licensed Canadian doctor who screens your full history and contraindications. clara never uses opioids.

When should I see someone in person instead?

Some signs need in person care, like a hot, red, severely swollen knee, a knee that locks or gives way, pain after a clear injury, or fever with joint pain. Our assessment screens for these, and if anything looks concerning we will tell you to seek in person care.
