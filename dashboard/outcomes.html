Clinical summary

Sarah Kim is a 45 year old female patient with a diagnosis of knee osteoarthritis (right knee). She is 92 days into her knee OA plan.
Treatments: C2 compound cream, naproxen, strengthening and walking program.

Pain and function

Daily pain has fallen from a baseline average of 6.4 to 3.2, a 50% reduction that crosses the 30% meaningful improvement bar.
PEG-3 interference improved from 6.7 to 4.3 and KOOS-12 function rose from 38 to 54, both beyond published meaningful change thresholds.

Medications

Oral medication use halved from 12 to 6 days per month, with zero opioid exposure on every month of record.

Work and daily life

Work absence fell from 6 hours to 1 hour per week, roughly 7 productive hours recovered weekly.
Five of seven functional milestones regained. Mood and sleep instruments are within normal limits.

Adherence and patient verdict

Adherence is strong (cream 86%, exercises 71%), though exercise dips on higher pain days; consider reinforcing the flare plan.
Patient rates herself Much better and scores clara 8 of 10.

Plan

Continue C2. Encourage exercise consistency. Review a naproxen taper at the next quarterly check-in.
