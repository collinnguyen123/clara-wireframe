Nerve pain · Other neuropathy

Other nerve pain

Nerve pain can also linger after surgery or an injury, or follow cancer treatment. For pain in one area, a cream can work right where it hurts.

From your clara care team⏱ 5 min readEvidence based

What it is

Sometimes nerve pain lingers in a scar or a limb for months after an operation or injury, which is called chronic post-surgical pain when it lasts beyond about three months without another cause.(1)

Nerve pain can also follow chemotherapy, or come from other nerve damage. These pains often have the same burning, tingling or shooting quality as other neuropathic pain.(2)

How a compound cream can help

Some evidence for a cream

When the pain is focused and well-defined, it is a reasonable fit for a cream that works at the surface with little reaching the rest of the body.(3)

Topical lidocaine and high-strength capsaicin are used, as are compounded amitriptyline, ketamine and lidocaine mixtures, and cancer-care guidelines list compounded creams as an option for nerve pain after chemotherapy.(4, 2)

A cream usually gives partial relief, is not FDA approved, and works best for focused pain rather than pain spread over a large area, so a clara doctor will judge the fit.(5)

Using a nerve-pain cream safely

Never apply a compound cream to broken, cut, raw or burned skin, keep it away from your eyes, do not cover it with tight dressings or heat, and store it out of reach of children. Mild stinging, warmth or redness where you apply it is common and usually settles. A clara doctor reviews every ingredient against your health, allergies and other medicines before anything is made.(5)
